# Snapshot 03 — Stack on Breach Point

**Timecode:** 0:12 – 0:18
**Beat:** Alpha has already slipped through the west pedestrian gate of compound `AC-1` and crossed the inner courtyard. Now tight stack discipline on the threshold of the main residence `A1`: the Breacher rigs the door, the Squad Leader holds at the rear with a calm face, and the AI silently compresses a 4-word operator whisper into a one-line standby call to the entry team. The viewer reads absolute control — no radio chatter, no wasted motion, the team is one organism pressed against the wall.

## Camera & Movement
- **Primary:** GoPro helmet-cam rigged to the #1 man (A1, the operator, on point) at the front of the Alpha stack, 16mm-equivalent, slight fisheye, T1.8 look.
- Handheld 1–2° micro-shake synced to the operator's breathing; faint helmet-rim vignette curves the top and bottom of the frame.
- Rolling-shutter smear on the pulsing IR strobes of the operators ahead; mild moiré in the NVG tube glow.
- Lens catches a thin crescent of condensed breath against the inside edge of the ballistic eye-pro every ~2 s.
- **ONE quick cut at ~0:15 (≈1.5 s of runtime):** over-the-shoulder on the Squad Leader at the rear of the stack — 35mm-equivalent, T2, shallow DOF — his calm face visible in profile, jaw set, orange IR chemlight glowing hot on the back of his helmet just off-frame right, four operators racked tight in front of him falling into bokeh, the weathered cream stucco face of main residence `A1` and the blurred silhouette of the white Toyota Hilux in the courtyard behind him. Match-cut back to the helmet-cam before the detonator click.
- Framing in the primary shot: the scarred wood-and-metal front door of main residence `A1` fills the right third, the Breacher kneeling at the jamb in the middle-ground, three stack-mates compressed against the weathered cream stucco wall in the near-ground left, NVG tubes bleeding faint green spill into the vignette, a slice of inner courtyard visible just behind the tail of the stack — the white Toyota Hilux parked askew, the rusted diesel generator shed in silhouette, the single-story annex `A2` squat in the SW corner, and distant low Bravo silhouettes still flowing toward the SE wall in the deep background.

## Action in Frame
The 5-man Alpha stack is compressed tight against the **weathered cream stucco exterior wall of main residence `A1`**, shoulders kissing the concrete-and-stucco, boots staggered in a zipper line through the ankle-height dust drift on the hard-packed dirt of the inner courtyard. They have already infiltrated through the narrow west pedestrian gate of compound `AC-1` and crossed a portion of the courtyard to reach this door.

- **Breacher (#2 in the stack)** kneels at the door jamb in a low crouch. His left hand holds a small clacker-style detonator with a coiled wire running up to a **linear breaching charge** taped vertically along the seam of the scarred wood-and-metal front door of `A1` — strip charge, flat det-cord, layered water-tamper bag, black gaffer tape crossing the hinges. His right hand smooths the last piece of tape.
- **#1 man (A1 operator, on point, the helmet-cam host)** stands directly behind the Breacher, body canted against the wall. His gloved right hand (tan Mechanix M-Pact) rests flat on the Breacher's right shoulder plate — the universal "set, ready when you are" touch. His suppressed 10.3" URG-I M4 hangs muzzle-down on its MS4 sling across his chest.
- **A2, TL-A, and SL** are racked in behind A1 in a tight diamond, each with a gloved hand on the back plate of the operator ahead. Suppressed 10.3" URG-I M4s (SureFire SOCOM Mini 2 cans) are angled low and outboard, muzzles down. DBAL-I2 IR lasers are active but invisible to the naked eye — only the faintest green spill bleeds through the NVG tubes in frame.
- **NVGs are flipped DOWN** on every helmet in this shot — four GPNVG-18 panoramic arrays, sixteen tubes total, each glowing a faint phosphor green.
- **IR strobes** pulse on every operator's back plate — small, sharp amber-green flashes in sync, roughly 1 Hz. Anamorphic horizontal flares streak across the frame on each pulse.
- The Breacher's kit visible in detail: multicam-black Crye G3 sleeves pushed up slightly at the cuff, dangler pouch hanging below the JPC 2.0, a single strip of breaching tape stuck to his left thigh ready-to-pull, a Safariland Glock 19 on his right hip.
- Thin ribbons of ankle-height dust drift curl around boots and the bottom of `A1`'s door. Courtyard context sits just beyond the tail of the stack: the battered **white Toyota Hilux** parked askew, the **rusted diesel generator shed** in silhouette, the **chicken coop** and weathered **vegetable patch**, and the squat single-story **annex `A2`** in the SW corner. On the far SE side of the courtyard, tiny distant low silhouettes of the Bravo element are still flowing toward the inner face of the SE perimeter wall in the deep background. The weak orange circle of the south-gate bare incandescent bulb bleeds around the corner of `A1` from off-frame right. High above on the NW corner of `A1`'s third-story/flat-roof parapet line, the single dim amber rectangle of the curtained 2nd-floor NW window is just visible as a smear of warm light at the top-right edge.
- Cut frame (Squad Leader OTS): his face is partially lit by the dim amber glow of his own HUD reflection in the clear ballistic eye-pro; boom mic pressed close to the corner of his mouth; lips barely moving as he counts down under his breath; behind him the weathered cream stucco of `A1` gives way to the darker courtyard, Hilux and generator shed falling into bokeh.

## Diegetic HUD Overlay (holographic, layered ON the image)
The HUD is a **transparent holographic AR layer projected directly onto the frame** — as if rendered by the helmet-cam operator's own optic suite. It is never a split-screen panel, never a solid block. HUD never covers the Breacher's hands, the charge, the door, any operator's face or muzzle — glyphs float in the negative space of stucco wall, dust, and courtyard.
