## Narrative Purpose
This 6-second bridge is the single visual hinge that converts **decision into deployment**. Shot 06 closed on the intimate 85mm portrait of the Squad Leader issuing the reroute order — `"team 1 upstairs, team 2 hold landing, medic stage base"` — a private, interior, almost meditative moment of command. Shot 07 opens on the opposite pole: a pure dead-vertical bird's-eye X-ray cutaway of main residence `A1` with three distinct sub-elements already flowing into their newly-assigned positions.

The transition must sell that the compact spoken sentence we just heard is *the same event* as the bodies now moving on the schematic. It is TacNet's thesis rendered as one unbroken camera move: **a human intent leaves one man's mouth, is parsed into three targeted routes, and instantly becomes kinetic.** The bridge earns the product claim by refusing to cut — we rise, literally, from the source of the order to the live picture of its execution, without a jump.

It is also the film's biggest scale shift. Every shot around it is intimate (85mm portraits, helmet-cam, thermal scope, OTS). This bridge is the only moment in the 60 seconds where the viewer is yanked vertically across ~30 meters of altitude through the roof of the target building. That spectacle is intentional: it re-establishes compound `AC-1` geometry in the viewer's mind (inner courtyard, white Hilux, generator shed, annex `A2`, south gate bulb, distant ridge with `OVER`) so that the bird's-eye of shot 07 reads instantly, and it allows the HUD command-tree in the top-right to animate its ACK sequence in clean real time.
